

How long does it take cure Anxiety and Depression?
How long does it take for anxiety and depression treatments to work?
There is no fixed timeline to fully "cure" anxiety or depression, as recovery looks different for everyone. However, most people start feeling noticeably better within 4 to 8 weeks of starting  medication. Your personal timeline will depend on your unique symptoms and how consistently you follow your treatment plan.

There is no single timeline because everyone’s body and mind respond differently. However, most treatments—like therapy, medication, or a combination of both—typically take 4 to 8 weeks to show noticeable, steady improvements. Some people may feel slight shifts earlier, while others need a bit more time to find the right treatment fit.

When can I visit the clinic?

Visits to our clinic are strictly by prior appointment. Psychiatric evaluations and psychological sessions require detailed clinical interviews and careful observation, lasting anywhere from 20 minutes to an hour per individual. To ensure every patient receives this high level of dedicated, unhurried care, our psychiatrists and clinical psychologists see a limited number of individuals each day. Please contact our administration team to schedule your appointment before visiting.

How do we diagnose mental health conditions?

Our clinical psychologists and psychiatrists conduct thorough, compassionate clinical interviews with each patient. We utilize validated diagnostic tools and carefully observe clinical symptoms to ensure an accurate diagnosis before creating a treatment plan.


Does our clinic provide emergency psychiatric treatment?

No, our clinic does not provide emergency psychiatric services. We operate strictly as an outpatient consultation service by appointment. If you or a loved one are experiencing a mental health crisis or a life-threatening emergency, please visit the nearest hospital emergency room or contact a national crisis helpline immediately.


Do we treat behavioral issues in children?
Yes, we provide comprehensive treatment for a wide range of childhood behavioral and developmental challenges. Our services address difficulties such as lack of focus at school, academic struggles, communication barriers, learning difficulties, and behavioral concerns like gaming addiction. 


Do we treat ADHD in adults?
Yes, we provide specialized diagnosis and treatment for adult ADHD using clinically validated assessments. Research indicates that roughly 2% of the global population has ADHD, which translates to over 20 million (2 crore) individuals in India alone. Due to a historical lack of mental health awareness, many adults live with undiagnosed ADHD, constantly struggling to manage their symptoms. To cope, they often "mask" their difficulties, hiding severe focus and executive functioning issues. This hidden struggle frequently leads to underperformance at work, leaving individuals vulnerable to unfair criticism for a condition that can be effectively treated.
